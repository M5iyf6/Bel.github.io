Castles of Belarus

early 16th century

Mir Castle

It is located in the village of Mir in the Grodno region of Belarus. In 1568, the castle passed into the possession of Nikolai Radziwill, who completed it in the Renaissance style.During the Napoleonic Wars, the castle was seriously damaged and stood in ruins for more than a century.In 2000, UNESCO added the Mir Castle to the World Cultural and Natural Heritage List.

Nesvizh Castle

Nesvizh is located in the Minsk region, 112 km southwest of Minsk.In the first half of the 16th century, one of the richest in Europe, the Radziwill family, became the owner of Nesvizh.After the Second World War, a sanatorium was located in the Nesvizh Castle, the park fell into disrepair.In 2005, the Nesvizh Palace and Park Complex was included in the UNESCO World Cultural Heritage List.

1323

Lida Castle

The city of Lida is located in the Grodno region, 112 km northeast of Grodno. The construction of the castle was started in 1323 by the Grand Duke of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania Gediminas. During its history, the castle has withstood many battles and sieges. In the XVIII century. it lost its strategic importance and began to gradually collapse.

Old and New castles in Grodno

The stone castle was built in 1398 by order of Prince Vytautas on the site of a former wooden fortress.In 1708 the building was severely damaged by the Swedes, after which it was not possible to use it.

Novogrudok castle

the castle, once located in the city of Novogrudok, was one of the key strongholds and one of the most powerful fortresses in the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, now its ruins are located on the slopes of the castle hill. A unique monument of defensive architecture of the Middle Ages, the ancient center of Novogrudok, the residence of the Grand Dukes of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania. It existed from the 13th to the 17th centuries. The foundations of fortifications with buildings and the ruins of towers have been preserved.

Holidays

Maslenitsa

This bright colorful holiday at the end of winter pleases both kids and adults. Games, songs, dances, cheerful laughter and noise are its main signs. And it's called Maslenitsa.

Easter

One of the most important Christian holidays in Belarus is Easter (or Vialikzen). This holiday is associated with a large number of different rituals and traditions, many of which are still performed today.

Ivan Kupala Day

This bright colorful holiday at the end of winter pleases both kids and adults. Games, songs, dances, cheerful laughter and noise are its main signs. And it's called Shrovetide.
